CHANDRASHEKAR AZAD

Chandrashekhar Azad, also known as Azad, was a brave and fearless freedom fighter in India's struggle for independence. Born in 1906, he was deeply committed to the cause of freeing India from British rule. Azad played a crucial role in the fight for independence and became a symbol of resistance against colonial oppression.
Azad was deeply influenced by the ideals of Mahatma Gandhi and joined the Non-Cooperation Movement at a young age. However, he later felt that peaceful protests were not enough to achieve India's freedom. He became a prominent member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA), advocating for more militant actions against the British.
Despite facing constant surveillance and threats from the British authorities, Azad remained steadfast in his commitment to the cause. He played a key role in several revolutionary activities, including the Kakori Train Robbery and the shooting of British officer J.P. Saunders. Azad's courage, determination, and sacrifice continue to inspire generations of Indians to fight for justice and freedom.
