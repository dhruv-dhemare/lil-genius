MODERN EARTH

This refers to the current geological epoch, the Holocene, which began around 11,700 years ago and continues to the present day

It's characterized by relatively stable climatic conditions compared to earlier epochs, allowing for the rise of human civilization and significant changes to the Earth's ecosystems due to human activity.

The modern age is furthur divided into sub classes such as the stone age,bronze age,copper age and the iron age

(i)THE STONE AGE

This period lasted from around 2.5 million years ago to about 3,000 BCE. During this time, humans primarily used stone tools and implements.

(ii)THE COPPER AGE

The Copper Age represents a transitional phase where humans began to use copper for tools and ornaments, but had not yet mastered the alloying of copper with tin to produce bronze.

(iii)THE BRONZE AGE

the Iron Age saw the widespread use of iron tools and weapons, marking a significant advancement due to iron's strength and abundance.

Following the Copper Age, the Bronze Age saw the widespread use of bronze, an alloy of copper and tin, for tools, weapons, and other artifacts.
